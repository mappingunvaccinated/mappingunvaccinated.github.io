Mapping the unvaccinated



The big cities of England such as London, Birmingham or Manchester have the lowest rates of people fully vaccinated, and so do their surroundings.

The amount of citizens who has received the second jab is bigger in rural areas, while the urban are reluctant to achieve the 70% promised to reach the 'herd immunity', now in doubt because of the Delta variant of the virus. Moreover, while the rest of developed countries are jabbing their youth, Great Britain is now vaccinating 16 and 17-year-old teens and moving carefully.

Furthermore, this pattern not only appears in the youth but also in elderly citizens: while about 94% of more-than-70-year-olds in England have had two jabs, in London City the percentage is only about 75%.

Percentage of people with the second dose. Darker color indicates higher percentage of people fully vaccinated against Covid-19. Yellow shows the local authorities where there is less percentage with the second shot.




The percentage of citizens already vaccinated with the second dose is 75.5% across England. The most populated cities are quite far from these values. The North West and the South East of England host regions where the percentage is around 60%. Westminster, close to City of London, shows the lowest average among the most populated cities. In Westminster, only one out of two citizens is fully vaccinated.

In the other hand, one of the larger cities that does present a high rate is Sunderland. With a population of 335,415, 77% of its citizens have a second dose, far away from the rest of the most inhabited cities in the country.






As shown in the map above, it is not only the city centres that present the lowest percentages. Local authorities close to the big cities are also reluctant to receive the second dose. This is the case of Greater London. While in Hackney and City of London 53.3% of population is fully vaccinated, this value drops to 49.9% in Westminster. Newham, with a 49.5%, has the lowest rate not only of Grater London, but also of the entire country.
